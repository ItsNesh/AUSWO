Student Visa (Subclass 500) - Everything You Need to Know

Last Updated: October 2025

The Student Visa (Subclass 500) is your gateway to studying in Australia at a world-class educational institution. This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about applying for, maintaining, and making the most of your Student Visa in Australia.

What is the Student Visa (Subclass 500)?

The Student Visa (Subclass 500) allows international students to live, study, and work in Australia for the duration of their studies at a Commonwealth Register of Institutions and Courses for Overseas Students (CRICOS) registered institution. This visa enables you to pursue a wide range of educational opportunities, from English language courses to university degrees and vocational training.

Who Can Apply?

The Student Visa is available to international students who have been accepted into a full-time course of study at a registered Australian educational institution. You can apply whether you're planning to undertake primary or secondary school education, English language courses, vocational education and training, higher education degrees, or postgraduate research.

Eligibility Requirements

Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE)

Before applying for your Student Visa, you must have received a Confirmation of Enrolment from your chosen Australian educational institution. This document confirms that you've been accepted into a registered course and have paid your tuition deposit. Your CoE includes important details such as your course name, duration, and commencement date.

Genuine Temporary Entrant (GTE) Requirement

You must demonstrate that you genuinely intend to stay in Australia temporarily for the purpose of studying. This requirement involves providing a written statement explaining your reasons for choosing to study in Australia, your chosen course, and how this study will benefit your future career prospects. You'll need to show ties to your home country and explain your intentions to return after completing your studies.

English Language Proficiency

Demonstrating adequate English language skills is essential for your Student Visa application. The required level varies depending on your course and institution, but most universities require an IELTS score of at least 5.5 to 6.5, or equivalent scores in other accepted tests such as TOEFL iBT, PTE Academic, or Cambridge English.

Some exemptions apply if you're a passport holder from certain English-speaking countries or if you've completed at least five years of study in English at secondary level or higher.

Financial Capacity

You must prove that you have sufficient funds to cover your tuition fees, living expenses, and return travel to Australia. As a general guide, you need to show evidence of approximately AUD 24,505 per year for living costs, plus your course fees and travel expenses. This can be demonstrated through bank statements, loan approvals, or financial guarantee letters from parents or sponsors.

Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC)

All international students must have adequate health insurance for the entire duration of their stay in Australia. Overseas Student Health Cover provides access to medical and hospital services in Australia and must be arranged before your visa application. Your educational institution can usually help you arrange appropriate OSHC.

Health and Character Requirements

All Student Visa applicants must meet Australia's health and character standards. You'll need to undergo health examinations by an approved panel physician, and these examinations typically include a general medical check-up, chest X-ray, and additional tests if required based on your country of origin or intended course of study.

For character requirements, you must provide police clearance certificates from all countries where you've lived for 12 months or more in the past 10 years since turning 16. You must also declare any criminal convictions or pending charges.

Application Process

The application process for a Student Visa must be completed online through the Department of Home Affairs website. Begin by creating an ImmiAccount and gathering all necessary documents, including your CoE, passport, English test results, financial evidence, and OSHC policy details.

Complete the online application form carefully, ensuring all information is accurate and truthful. Upload all required documents in the specified formats, and pay the visa application fee using a credit or debit card. After submission, you may be asked to provide biometrics at an Australian Visa Application Centre or undergo health examinations.

Processing times vary depending on your country of origin and the time of year, but most applications are processed within one to four months. You can track your application progress through your ImmiAccount.

Visa Conditions and Work Rights

The Student Visa comes with specific conditions that you must follow throughout your stay in Australia. You're permitted to work up to 48 hours per fortnight during your study periods and unlimited hours during scheduled course breaks. This work right begins only after your course has commenced.

You must maintain satisfactory course attendance and academic progress, which typically means attending at least 80% of your scheduled classes and passing your subjects. You're required to maintain adequate health insurance throughout your stay and must notify your institution and the Department of Home Affairs of any changes to your address within seven days.

Visa Duration and Extension

Your Student Visa is typically granted for the duration of your course plus a short additional period. For courses lasting more than 10 months that end in November or December, you'll usually receive visa validity until March 15 of the following year. For courses ending between January and October, you'll typically receive an additional two months after course completion.

If you need to extend your stay to complete your studies or undertake further education, you can apply for a new Student Visa while in Australia. You must apply before your current visa expires and provide evidence of your new enrolment and continued compliance with visa conditions.

Family Members and Dependents

Your spouse or de facto partner and dependent children can be included in your Student Visa application. They'll receive the same visa validity period as you and will have limited work rights. Your partner can work up to 48 hours per fortnight, and dependent children can attend school in Australia.

Pathways After Graduation

Completing your studies on a Student Visa opens up several pathways for your future in Australia. Many graduates choose to apply for the Temporary Graduate Visa (Subclass 485), which allows you to work in Australia for two to four years depending on your qualification level.

During your studies, you may also become eligible for skilled migration pathways, particularly if you study in a field listed on Australia's skilled occupation lists. Some graduates secure employer sponsorship, while others may qualify for state or territory nomination programs.

Important Tips for Success

To ensure a smooth experience on your Student Visa, apply well in advance of your intended course start date, ideally at least three to four months before. Keep copies of all important documents including your visa grant letter, CoE, and OSHC policy details.

Maintain open communication with your educational institution's international student support office, as they can provide valuable guidance on visa matters and student obligations. Stay informed about your visa conditions and ensure you understand your work rights limitations and course progress requirements.

Plan your finances carefully and consider opening an Australian bank account soon after arrival. Take advantage of student support services offered by your institution, including academic support, counseling, and career guidance.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Many students encounter problems by exceeding their work hour limitations during study periods or failing to maintain satisfactory course attendance and progress. Avoid letting your OSHC lapse, as this is a breach of your visa conditions and can result in visa cancellation.

Don't wait until the last minute to apply for visa extensions if you need to continue your studies. Ensure you keep the Department of Home Affairs informed of any changes to your circumstances, including changes in your course, institution, or address.
