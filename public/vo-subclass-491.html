Skilled Work Regional (Provisional) Visa (Subclass 491): A Pathway to Permanent Residency via Regional Australia

Last Updated: October 2025

The Skilled Work Regional (Provisional) Visa (Subclass 491) is a five-year provisional visa for skilled workers who want to live and work in regional Australia. This visa provides a pathway to permanent residency through the Subclass 191 visa and offers significant points advantages for applicants willing to commit to regional areas.

What Makes This Visa Unique

The Subclass 491 is specifically designed to encourage skilled migration to regional areas of Australia. You receive 15 bonus points for state or territory government nomination or 10 points for nomination by an eligible family member. This substantial points boost makes it accessible to applicants who might not qualify for other skilled visas. After three years of living and working in regional Australia and meeting income requirements, you can apply for permanent residency through the Subclass 191 visa.

Key Benefits

This visa allows you to live, work, and study in designated regional areas of Australia for up to five years. You can include family members in your application and travel to and from Australia multiple times. You gain access to Medicare and have a clear pathway to permanent residency. The visa also offers more generous points allocation, making it easier to receive an invitation compared to metropolitan-focused visas.

Understanding Regional Australia

Regional Australia includes all of Australia except Sydney, Melbourne, and Brisbane. Major regional cities that are eligible include Perth, Adelaide, Gold Coast, Newcastle, Wollongong, Geelong, Hobart, Canberra, Sunshine Coast, and Darwin. These areas offer excellent lifestyle opportunities, lower living costs, strong job markets, and quality education and healthcare facilities.

Two Pathways to Nomination

State or Territory Government Nomination

Each state and territory government maintains their own occupation lists and nomination criteria for regional areas. This pathway provides 15 points toward your points test. States actively recruit skilled workers to fill gaps in their regional labor markets and may offer additional support to help you settle in regional areas.

Family Sponsorship

An eligible family member who is an Australian citizen, permanent resident, or eligible New Zealand citizen living in regional Australia can nominate you. The family member must be at least 18 years old and have lived in a designated regional area for at least 12 months. Eligible family members include parents, siblings, children, aunts, uncles, nieces, nephews, or grandparents. This pathway provides 10 points toward your points test.

Basic Eligibility Requirements

You must be under 45 years of age when invited to apply and have an occupation on the relevant skilled occupation list. A positive skills assessment is required, along with at least 65 points including nomination points. You need to demonstrate competent English and be nominated by a state or territory government or sponsored by an eligible family member. You must also receive an invitation to apply through SkillSelect.

The Application Process

Begin by obtaining your skills assessment and achieving the required English test scores. Research which states or territories nominate your occupation in regional areas and determine if you have eligible family members in regional Australia who could sponsor you. Submit your Expression of Interest through SkillSelect, indicating your interest in the Subclass 491 visa. Once nominated or sponsored, you'll receive an invitation to apply and have 60 days to lodge your complete visa application including all supporting documents.

Points Calculation

Points are awarded based on age, English language ability, skilled employment experience, educational qualifications, and partner skills. The crucial advantage is the 15 bonus points for state nomination or 10 points for family sponsorship. This significant boost means applicants with lower base scores can still qualify, making regional migration more accessible.

Visa Conditions and Requirements

You must live, work, and study only in designated regional areas of Australia. This is a mandatory condition for the full five years of the visa. You cannot move to Sydney, Melbourne, or Brisbane during this period. You're required to notify the Department of Home Affairs of certain changes including residential address, employment details, and passport information.

Pathway to Permanent Residency

After holding your Subclass 491 visa for at least three years, you may be eligible to apply for the permanent Subclass 191 visa. To qualify, you must have complied with all visa conditions, lived in regional Australia for at least three years, and earned at least the minimum income threshold in each of three years. The income threshold is set annually and must come from taxable income in regional Australia.

Processing Times and Costs

State or territory nomination processing typically takes 6 to 12 weeks. Visa processing after invitation usually takes 8 to 13 months. Costs include the state nomination fee, which varies by state and territory, the main applicant visa application charge, additional charges for family members, and expenses for skills assessment, English tests, health examinations, and police certificates.

Work and Study Rights

You have full work rights in regional areas and can work in any occupation or for any employer within designated regional zones. You can also study at any educational institution in regional Australia. Many regional areas have excellent universities and TAFE institutions offering diverse courses.

Benefits of Regional Living

Regional Australia offers many advantages including lower cost of living compared to major cities, shorter commute times and less traffic congestion, strong and growing job markets with skills shortages, family-friendly communities with excellent schools, beautiful natural environments and outdoor lifestyle opportunities, and easier pathways to permanent residency. Many regional cities offer big-city amenities with a relaxed lifestyle.

State Nomination Priorities

Different states have different priorities for regional nomination. South Australia offers broad opportunities across Adelaide and regional SA. Tasmania actively recruits for various occupations statewide. Western Australia includes Perth in the regional designation. Queensland focuses on regional areas outside Brisbane. New South Wales targets regional areas with specific skill needs. Victoria promotes regional centers like Geelong and Ballarat. The Northern Territory offers opportunities in Darwin and regional NT.

Tips for a Successful Application

Research regional areas thoroughly to find the best fit for your skills and lifestyle. Consider cities like Adelaide, Perth, or Hobart that offer metropolitan amenities but are classified as regional. Demonstrate genuine commitment to living in regional Australia through job research, accommodation planning, and community connections. Ensure you understand the income requirements for transitioning to permanent residency. Keep detailed records of your employment and income in regional Australia from day one. Connect with regional settlement services and community groups to help with your transition. Consider the long-term benefits of regional living, including better work-life balance and community integration.
